## Direct Competitors

### Flippd (Closest Competitor)
- **What:** Mobile-first inventory tracker for resellers. Tracks inventory, profit, expenses, mileage.
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Web app
- **Pricing:** Free (20 items, basically a demo) / Pro $9.99/month or $99.99/year
- **Traction:** ~66 App Store ratings total after 3+ years. Estimated 3,000-15,000 total users across all platforms. Zero Reddit presence. Nobody in reseller Discord groups mentions them.
- **Strengths:** QR labels, receipt scanning (AI), mileage tracking, offline mode, CSV import
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Free tier is a bait-and-switch (20 items = useless)
  - No dashboard / at-a-glance view
  - Basic reports only (no custom report builder)
  - No ADHD-friendly design thinking
  - No Kanban / visual pipeline
  - Profit doesn't subtract expenses
  - Virtually zero community presence or word-of-mouth
  - No electronics-specific features

### Vendoo
- **What:** Primarily a cross-listing tool (post one listing to 10+ platforms). Inventory is a side feature.
- **Pricing:** $19.99-$69.99/month
- **Strengths:** Cross-listing saves hours of work. Large user base among high-volume sellers.
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive. Aimed at high-volume sellers, not casual flippers. Auto-delist only works on 6 of 11 platforms.

### List Perfectly
- **What:** Cross-listing + inventory
- **Pricing:** $29-$69/month
- **Weaknesses:** Similar to Vendoo — cross-listing focused, expensive, not built for small-time flippers.

### Crosslist
- **What:** Cross-listing + basic inventory
- **Pricing:** Similar range to above

### Flyp
- **What:** Consignment-style — they sell for you
- **Pricing:** Commission-based

## Indirect Competitors

- **Intuit/QuickBooks, Zoho** — overkill for resellers, not reseller-specific
- **Sortly, inFlow** — generic inventory apps, don't understand reselling

## Strategic Advantages to Pursue

### 1. Generous Free Tier
200-300 items free (vs Flippd's 20). Pro at $5-7/month for advanced features. Undercut every competitor.

### 2. Tax Season / Schedule C Export
Huge unserved pain point. 1099-K reports gross sales, not profit. Resellers overpay taxes or scramble at year-end. A tax-ready summary export would be a killer feature nobody else offers.

### 3. Community-Led Growth
User is already in reseller Discord groups. Flippd has zero community presence. Being the founder who's in the room is more valuable than any ad spend. Target r/flipping, r/Ebay, r/reselling on Reddit.

### 4. Death Pile Aging & Nudges
Visual aging system (green > yellow > red), capital-tied-up tracking, weekly nudges. Emotional hook that builds daily habit.

### 5. Electronics-Specific Intelligence
Depreciation awareness, model/spec fields (storage, processor, carrier), platform guidance. Position as THE app for tech flippers.

### 6. Spreadsheet Migration
Import from Google Sheets. Position against spreadsheets, not against Flippd. Most early users will switch from a spreadsheet or from nothing.

### 7. ADHD-Friendly Brand Identity
"Built for brains that move fast." No competitor occupies this identity. Resonates beyond just ADHD — everyone prefers clean, clear, non-overwhelming UI.

---

## Key Takeaway

The market is big ($200B+), growing fast (11-13%/yr), and **nobody has won the small-time flipper category.** Flippd is the closest competitor with maybe 10K users after 3+ years — weak traction. The opportunity is real. The challenge is distribution and community, not building features.
